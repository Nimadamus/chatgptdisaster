Timeline of Events

December 1, 2025 - Internal Memo

"Code Red" Declaration

An internal company memo declared a "code red" situation, instructing OpenAI staff to shift attention and resources. This preceded the next day's outage.

December 2, 2025 - 2:30 PM ET

Outage Begins

Users across multiple platforms—web, mobile app, and API—began reporting unresponsive chat interfaces. Many saw only a black dot when attempting to generate responses.

December 2, 2025 - Peak Reports

3,000+ Users Report Issues

Downdetector logged approximately 3,000 user reports. Social media flooded with complaints from frustrated users unable to access ChatGPT.

December 2, 2025 - 3:12 PM ET

Mitigation Complete

OpenAI completed the mitigation rollout, restoring service. The company cited a "routing misconfiguration" as the cause.

The Bigger Picture: December 2025 Issues

The December 2nd outage wasn't an isolated incident. Throughout December 2025, users have reported:

  • Slow or laggy responses, especially with GPT-4 models
  • Subscription payment and activation errors
  • Image generation quality complaints
  • Memory and context retention failures
  • Login errors and access issues
  • API timeouts affecting businesses

An OpenAI Community post warned users to "expect various errors during December 2025" due to ongoing construction and maintenance work.

Frequently Asked Questions

Why did ChatGPT go down on December 2, 2025?

OpenAI reported the outage was caused by a "routing misconfiguration" that affected users worldwide starting around 2:30 PM ET.

How many users were affected?

According to Downdetector, approximately 3,000 users formally reported issues. However, with ChatGPT serving 300+ million weekly active users, the actual number affected was likely in the millions.

How long was ChatGPT down?

The outage lasted approximately 42 minutes, from 2:30 PM ET to 3:12 PM ET.

What is the "Code Red" declaration?

On December 1, 2025, an internal OpenAI memo declared a "code red" situation, instructing staff to shift attention and resources to address critical issues. This preceded the December 2nd outage.
