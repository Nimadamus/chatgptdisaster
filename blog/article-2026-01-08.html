Here's what the tech press isn't telling you.

AI can pattern-match at superhuman speed. What it can't do is understand context, exercise judgment, or take responsibility for its outputs. Those are still exclusively human capabilities.

The limitations of current AI aren't being solved by more parameters or more training data. The fundamental architecture has ceiling that no amount of scale will break through.

When an AI system fails, there's no accountability. The company blames the user. The user blames the tool. And the actual harm falls on whoever trusted the output.

The bottom line: approach AI claims with healthy skepticism. The technology has real uses, but it's not the magic solution the industry wants you to believe.
