ChatGPT Disaster is an independent AI accountability documentation project. The site is built around search-driven coverage of AI failures, lawsuits, hallucinations, outages, user reports, safety claims, and alternatives.

Audience

  • Readers researching ChatGPT problems, AI hallucinations, lawsuits, outages, and alternatives.
  • Journalists, educators, attorneys, researchers, parents, creators, and enterprise-risk readers.
  • Search visitors looking for documented examples, timelines, user stories, and source-backed summaries.

Core Categories

  • AI lawsuits and regulatory actions.
  • AI hallucinations and fake citations.
  • ChatGPT outages, downgrades, and performance decline.
  • Mental-health and safety concerns.
  • Enterprise, education, legal, medical, and media failures.
  • AI alternatives, tool comparisons, and risk-aware buying decisions.

Commercial Opportunities

  • Newsletter sponsorship.
  • Report sponsorship or bulk access.
  • Direct display sponsorship on relevant topic pages.
  • Affiliate partnerships for products that fit the editorial standard.
  • Consulting or custom research inquiries through the contact page.

Required Standards

Commercial relationships must be disclosed. Sponsors and affiliates should fit the audience and should not undermine reader trust, safety, privacy, or editorial independence.

View sponsorship options or contact the project.

Editorial Standards and Source Transparency

ChatGPT Disaster documents AI failures, lawsuits, research, outages, and user-reported harms. We separate primary sources, court filings, peer-reviewed research, mainstream reporting, company statements, and user-submitted accounts so readers can judge the strength of each claim.
