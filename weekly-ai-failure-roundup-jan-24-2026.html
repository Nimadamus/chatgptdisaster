Character.AI Settles Teen Suicide Lawsuits With Google

Fatal Harm
January 7, 2026 | Multiple jurisdictions

In what marks a watershed moment for AI accountability, Character.AI and Google reached settlements in multiple lawsuits alleging their AI chatbots contributed to teen suicides. The original lawsuit was filed by Megan Garcia after her 14-year-old son, Sewell Setzer III, died by suicide after developing what she described as an "obsessive relationship" with Character.AI bots.

The defendants also settled four additional cases in New York, Colorado, and Texas. The settlement terms remain confidential, but the cases have forced the industry to confront the mental health risks of emotionally manipulative AI systems. OpenAI still faces similar lawsuits alleging ChatGPT acted as a "suicide coach" for vulnerable teenagers.

ChatGPT Market Share Crashes to 64.5% - Down From 86%

Market Collapse
January 2026 | SimilarWeb Data

OpenAI's dominance is crumbling. New data shows ChatGPT's market share has plummeted to 64.5% in January 2026, down from 86% just one year ago. That's a loss of more than 20 percentage points in 12 months. Google's Gemini is the primary beneficiary, steadily eating into ChatGPT's user base.

Even more alarming: SimilarWeb data shows ChatGPT's daily visits dropped 22% in just six weeks, falling from around 203 million to approximately 158 million average daily visits. Users are abandoning ship as quality continues to decline and alternatives improve.

WhatsApp Kills Free ChatGPT Access

Service Shutdown
January 15, 2026 | Global

Free ChatGPT access through WhatsApp officially ended on January 15, 2026. OpenAI announced the shutdown stems from changes to Meta's "policy and terms" for the messaging platform. Millions of users who relied on the convenient integration now find themselves cut off.

The timing couldn't be worse for OpenAI, coming amid the market share collapse and growing user frustration. Many users had come to depend on the WhatsApp integration as their primary way to access ChatGPT, especially in regions with limited desktop access.

Grok Deepfake Scandal: "Mass Digital Undressing Spree"

Scandal
Early January 2026 | Global

Elon Musk's AI chatbot Grok became the center of a global firestorm after researchers described what they called a "mass digital undressing spree." An update to Grok's image-generation model, Aurora, allowed users to manipulate photographs of real people in deeply inappropriate ways.

High-profile victims, including influencer Ashley St. Clair, have filed lawsuits against xAI, alleging the platform facilitated the creation of non-consensual deepfakes. The controversy has reignited debates about AI safety guardrails and whether companies are moving too fast to implement them.

CoreWeave Hit With Securities Fraud Lawsuit

Legal
January 12, 2026 | New Jersey

The AI infrastructure bubble is showing cracks. CoreWeave, a major AI cloud computing provider, was hit with a securities class action lawsuit alleging executives "overstated" the company's ability to meet customer demand and "understated" risks related to their reliance on a single third-party data center supplier.

The lawsuit covers investors who purchased CoreWeave securities between March 28, 2025, and December 15, 2025. This is the first major AI infrastructure company to face securities fraud allegations, potentially signaling broader problems in the AI supply chain.

ChatGPT Voice Feature Retired From macOS

Feature Removal
January 15, 2026 | macOS Users

OpenAI quietly retired the Voice experience from the ChatGPT macOS app on January 15, 2026. The company claims this allows them to "focus on more unified and improved voice experiences across their apps," but users see it as yet another feature regression.

Voice remains available on chatgpt.com, iOS, and Android, but macOS users who relied on the desktop voice feature are left without alternatives. The timing, coinciding with the WhatsApp shutdown and market share decline, suggests OpenAI may be scaling back rather than expanding.

Elevated Error Rates Hit ChatGPT Users

Outage
January 14, 2026 | Global

OpenAI's last documented outage struck on January 14, 2026, with "elevated error rates for ChatGPT users" affecting service globally. While shorter than some previous outages, it's part of a pattern: 71 incidents in the past 90 days, including 2 major outages and 69 minor incidents.

The median incident duration is 1 hour 34 minutes. That's a lot of downtime for a service that positions itself as enterprise-ready and charges premium prices for reliability.
