90-Day Reliability Report

ChatGPT's reliability has been declining significantly:

  • 46 total incidents in the last 90 days
  • 1 major outage (complete service unavailability)
  • 45 minor incidents (degraded performance, elevated errors)
  • Median resolution time: 1 hour 54 minutes
  • Longest outage: 4+ days (December 26 - January 2)

For a service handling 800 million weekly users and charging $20/month for Plus, this level of unreliability is concerning many paying customers. If you're experiencing issues, see our troubleshooting guide.

What To Do When ChatGPT Is Down

Immediate Options:

  • Check status.openai.com for official updates
  • Check Downdetector for user reports
  • Try refreshing or clearing browser cache
  • Try a different browser or incognito mode
  • Wait 15-30 minutes and try again

Alternative AI Tools:

  • Claude (Anthropic) - Often available when ChatGPT is down
  • Google Gemini - Google's AI assistant
  • Microsoft Copilot - Uses GPT but different infrastructure
  • Perplexity AI - Good for research queries

See our full ChatGPT Alternatives Guide for more options.

Why Does ChatGPT Keep Going Down?

Several factors contribute to ChatGPT's reliability issues:

  • Massive scale: 800 million weekly users strain infrastructure
  • Rapid feature additions: New capabilities like GPT-5 introduced instability
  • Computational demands: LLMs require enormous processing power
  • Peak usage spikes: Business hours cause predictable overloads
  • Cost pressure: OpenAI reportedly loses money on each query

As OpenAI prioritizes growth and new features over stability, existing users bear the cost of an increasingly unreliable service.
